Why this feels different

Useful first It does the real task before teaching

You still get the answer you needed. The learning layer comes after the job is done, so the skill stays useful during normal work.

Daily practice It fits real conversations, not textbook drills

Instead of separate exercises, you improve through actual questions, requests, and work chat.

Low friction It works especially well for non-native speakers

You can practice consistently even when you are not explicitly in “study mode”. That is what makes it stick.

What happens on each turn?

1. Claude answers normally

No learning ritual before the answer. It handles the actual request first.

2. It corrects your English

Grammar, spelling, word choice, punctuation, or naturalness — whatever matters most.

3. It teaches one useful thing

A phrase, grammar tip, pattern, or more natural rephrasing you can reuse right away.

Who is it for?

Developers using Claude Code
  • You already type prompts and requests in English
  • You want improvement without leaving your workflow
Professionals who need practical English
  • You care about emails, work chat, documentation, and meetings
  • You prefer natural expressions over textbook sentences
Learners who struggle with consistency
  • You do not want another separate study app
  • You want progress to happen while doing useful work

Usage 
/english-coach

Then just keep chatting normally in English.

Mini example

dose this work for practice my english?
  • Answer: Claude answers the actual question first.
  • Correction: “Does this work for practicing my English?”
  • Teaching point: after “for”, use the -ing form in this sentence.
